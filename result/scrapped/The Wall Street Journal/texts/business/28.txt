I’M SORRY TO REPORT that Volvo has discontinued U.S. sales of its debonair V90 estate—its “low-roof” station wagon—in favor of the V90 Cross Country, which is effectively the same car only, ineffably, not as cool. Introduced in 2017, the CC sits on a slightly raised suspension (8.3 inches of ground clearance, AWD standard, air springs optional), and is fitted with all-season tires, plastic wheel-arch surrounds and dent-resistant lower-body cladding. It’s the wagon in wellies.
As if to answer the question before it was asked, Volvo’s press material notes that the company sold a mere 131 V90s in the first five months of 2021. The CC has not fared much better, with May 2022 year-to-date sales of 336 units. At that rate the CC may be called back to Valhalla as well.

SYDNEY—The defense ministers of Australia and China held their first meeting in three years on the sidelines of a conference in Singapore, talks that could herald a broader easing of tensions, including over trade.
Australian Defense Minister Richard Marles said the meeting with Chinese Defense Minister Gen. Wei Fenghe at the Shangri-La Dialogue, an annual defense conference, was a critical first step toward improving ties with China, Australia’s largest trading partner.

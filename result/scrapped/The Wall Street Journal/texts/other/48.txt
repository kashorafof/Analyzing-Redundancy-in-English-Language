BUFFALO, N.Y.—Several streets on the East Side of Buffalo are named after fruits, yet residents in this predominantly Black neighborhood have few places to buy them. 
Residents in the Fruit Belt area are struggling to buy groceries following a mass shooting at the neighborhood’s only supermarket last month that left 10 people dead. The racist massacre is a harsh reminder of socioeconomic neglect the community has decried for decades.

All golfers aspire to add more distance to their drive. The secret lies in developing hip strength and stability, range of motion in the lower and middle back, and stability through the shoulders, says Clive Brewer, director of athletic performance for OrthoArizona in Scottsdale and strength and conditioning coach of 20-time LPGA Tour winner Cristie Kerr. This training trifecta will help you generate more clubhead speed to hit longer drives, he says. 
The backswing involves rotation in the lower back, pelvis and hip joints. It also requires middle-back mobility and shoulder stability to enable the upper arm to rotate through the shot and transfer speed through the arm to the club.

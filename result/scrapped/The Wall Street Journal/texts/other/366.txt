It’s a summer of pay increases at many big U.S. companies.
To address inflation and to retain workers in what many executives say remains a tough labor market, some large employers are raising salaries or doling out special bonuses. Exxon Mobil gave U.S. employees a one-time payment in June equivalent to 3% of their salaries. PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP says it will hand out raises in July to reflect its performance in its fiscal year that just ended. Microsoft told employees this spring it planned to nearly double its global budget for merit-based raises.

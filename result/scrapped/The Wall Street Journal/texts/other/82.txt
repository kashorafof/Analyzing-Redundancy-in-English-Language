FAIRVIEW HEIGHTS, Ill.—The future of abortion access is beginning to take shape in places like this southern Illinois town, which is expected to become a destination for women from nearby states poised to outlaw abortion if Roe v. Wade is overturned.
Bracing for an influx of patients, Kawanna Shannon, director of patient access at Planned Parenthood’s regional logistics center in southern Illinois, said she and colleagues are trying to hire 15 more employees and stay open seven days a week, compared with six currently.

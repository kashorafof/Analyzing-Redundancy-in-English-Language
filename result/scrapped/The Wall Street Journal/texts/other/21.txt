CROMWELL, Conn.—With his sport in turmoil as the Saudi-backed LIV Golf series continues to poach golfers, PGA Tour commissioner Jay Monahan unveiled his first major counterattack and plans to incentivize players to stay on the tour. 
A day after meeting with players, Monahan outlined increased purse sizes, a new international series for the tour’s top stars and a revamped schedule. The changes are a direct response to the challenge mounted by LIV Golf, the rival series funded by Saudi Arabia’s sovereign-wealth fund that has lured superstars with lucrative appearance fees and historically rich prize money.

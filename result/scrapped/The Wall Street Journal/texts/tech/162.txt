A man charged with driving his car into a crowd in New York’s Times Square in 2017, killing one teenager and injuring more than 20 others, was found not responsible because of mental illness.
A spokesman for the Manhattan district attorney’s office confirmed that a jury on Wednesday found Richard Rojas not responsible by reason of mental disease or defect for all charges against him.

When Robert Willey’s son went from preschool into elementary school two years ago, Mr. Willey suddenly had a new problem at work: His child had a lot more days off from school than Mr. Willey had time off. He had to find a way to cover that gap.
It took some improvisation. During a weeklong break at his son’s school in February, for instance, Mr. Willey took afternoons off “in order to chauffeur him around without actively submitting for vacation days or going through HR protocols.”

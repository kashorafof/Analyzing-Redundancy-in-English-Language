“Flexibility” has become the mantra of the hybrid workplace: Employees will be happier if they are allowed to set their own hours. But a new study suggests that may not necessarily be true.
The study found that people working weekends and holidays enjoy their work less and experience decreased motivation—even when they choose the schedule themselves.

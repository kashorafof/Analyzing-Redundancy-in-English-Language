As humans, we spend a significant amount of our lives in states of task-irrelevant thought—commonly known as mind wandering. In fact, according to research, we spend almost half of our waking hours with a wandering mind, grappling with a range of thoughts about our past, present and future.
Mind wandering is especially prevalent in a round of golf, with the average golfer spending only 19 to 27 minutes on the process of actually hitting golf shots over the course of a typical four-hour round. That leaves an awful lot of time for a golfer’s mind to wander and interfere with the attentional demands of hitting successful shots. It is this task-irrelevant thought that can ignite feelings of anxiety and cause golfers to experience choking—especially when competing under pressure.

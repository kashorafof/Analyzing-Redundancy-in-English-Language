It’s no secret that resolving complicated issues over email can feel frustrating and taxing. But the cost of email reliance may be more far-reaching than previously known. New research suggests that compared with face-to-face conversation, email not only makes resolution more difficult, but can also worsen one’s performance on subsequent tasks.
“Not only do you have less motivation, but you also suffer cognitive deficits,” says Ravi Gajendran, the study’s lead author.

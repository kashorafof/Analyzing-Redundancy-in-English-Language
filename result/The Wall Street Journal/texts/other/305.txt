WIMBLEDON, England—There are days when Nick Kyrgios feels he needs to set the record straight. Sometimes he does it in a testy press conference, firing back at reporters while eating sushi, as he did here last week. And sometimes, he does it on the tennis court—occasionally grumbling—but mostly uncorking devilish shots to the farthest reaches of the court.
Monday on Centre Court was one of the latter days, a reminder that for all of his maddening habits, he remains a supremely gifted tennis player. And for the first time, since 2015, he is also a Grand Slam quarterfinalist. With his calmest performance of the tournament, Kyrgios outlasted the 20-year-old American Brandon Nakashima in five sets, 4-6, 6-4, 7-6(2), 3-6, 6-2.
